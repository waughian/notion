‘Known to the authorities.’ Hearing those words has become depressingly routine in the wake of an atrocity. And so it is with last summer’s Southport attack. Since Axel Rudakubana pleaded guilty yesterday to the murder of three young girls, we have learnt that he was referred to Prevent, the government’s counter-extremism programme, three times. Although it seems no terrorist motive was uncovered during the police investigation, and he’s not believed to subscribe to any particular ideology, the teenager was known to have an obsession with violence before he carried out his killing spree. So how did he slip through the net? Why were so many red flags ignored? These are the kinds of questions we must now discuss openly. We need a full, frank reckoning with this catastrophic state failure.
